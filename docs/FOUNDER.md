## Background

French-born, based in West Launceston, Tasmania. Self-taught developer and entrepreneur. Prior career as a photo editor at Agence Images (formerly Images du Sud, Saint-Laurent-du-Var, Côte d'Azur) — helped transition the agency from traditional film photography to digital Web2 imagery (~2000–2005). The company later became Jupiterimages (EU operations, Sophia Antipolis/Cannes) and was ultimately acquired by Getty Images. That analogue-to-digital transition is the direct pattern behind StudioLedger: same shift, now Web2→Web3, in the freelance creative economy.

Built ACI Capture (drone services business) approximately 5 years ago using the NEIS program — that business is unrelated to StudioLedger and no longer active.

StudioLedger started as a personal project to solve a real problem: creators getting stiffed on payments, platforms taking 10–20% cuts, and no portable proof of completed work. The same Web2-to-Web3 shift Remy saw in photography is now happening in the freelance creative economy. StudioLedger brings together agentic engineering, smart contract infrastructure, and the XRP Ledger to make that transition real. The idea evolved into a full XRPL-native platform with escrow protection, verifiable credentials, and radical fee transparency (0.98%).
